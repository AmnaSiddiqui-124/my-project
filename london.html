Today in London: International Summit starts at Westminster.

The 2026 Global Economic Summit has officially commenced at Westminster, bringing together leaders from over 40 nations to discuss the future of digital currencies and sustainable trade. Security is tight across Central London as delegates arrive for the opening ceremony. DNBC reporters on the ground suggest that this meeting could lead to the first major reform of international tax laws in over a decade.

Beyond the summit, London's transport network is seeing a massive overhaul. The Mayor's office has announced that 90% of the city's bus fleet is now fully electric, aiming for a net-zero carbon footprint by the end of the year. This move has been welcomed by environmental groups but has sparked debate among small business owners regarding the new congestion charges being implemented in the outer boroughs.

In the financial district, the City of London continues to dominate the European market despite global shifts. Real estate prices in the Docklands have seen a 15% surge as major tech firms move their headquarters to the UK. Experts believe that the integration of AI-driven legal services is making London the most efficient hub for international business law.

Finally, the cultural scene in London is buzzing with the reopening of several historic theaters in the West End after extensive renovations. Tourism is at an all-time high for March, with visitors flocking to see the latest immersive digital art exhibitions that have taken over the Southbank area.
